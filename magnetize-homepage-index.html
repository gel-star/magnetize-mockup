The real problem

Most PPC problems aren't Google's fault. They're structural.

In nearly every account we audit, the platform isn't the problem. It's what's been built on top of it — poor conversion data, structures that ignore commercial reality, and buying journeys the platforms never get to see.

01

Your data treats everything equally

A call from a qualified buyer is worth far more than a brochure download — but most accounts treat them the same, so Google chases the cheapest conversion.

What we doWe value each action by its real commercial impact and feed those values back, so bidding chases revenue.
02

One-size-fits-all campaigns

A £200 service and a £20,000 contract get treated identically, so budget flows to the easiest wins instead of the most profitable ones.

What we doWe structure at service-line level and use margin data to steer budget toward profit, not volume.
03

A journey the platform can't see

A patient researches for weeks. A B2B buyer loops in three departments. If your account tracks only the last click, all of it is invisible.

What we doWe track micro-conversions, CRM stages and offline sales, feeding the signals that actually predict revenue.
04

Budget goes where it's easiest

One branch is fully booked while another needs clients. A default structure can't see the difference, so spend lands in the wrong place.

What we doWe build around your locations, service lines and capacity, steering spend to what converts profitably now.
05

Demand the business can't serve

Calls go unanswered. Leads sit in the CRM. None of it shows in a PPC report, but all of it decides whether spend becomes revenue.

What we doWe match PPC to how you actually operate — schedules, lead types and targeting around real capacity.
06

Strong campaigns, weak landing pages

An ad promises value; a slow page with a long form undoes it in seconds, and paid traffic leaves before converting.

What we doWe sharpen the message and remove friction so the page delivers what the ad promised.
